## Title

The Black Saturday Lesson Most Cockatoo Decks Still Get Wrong: BAL Ratings, AS 3959, and the Composite Trap

## Subtitle

The 2009 Black Saturday bushfires reshaped Victorian decking standards forever. Sixteen years on, most carpenters in the Endeavour Hills Hills still don't know which materials actually pass BAL certification — and the building surveyor finds out at occupancy.

---

## Body

Cockatoo, Cranbourne Upper and Emerald sit in the same bushfire risk zone that took 11 lives on Black Saturday in February 2009. After the fires, Victoria rewrote AS 3959 — the construction standard for buildings in bushfire-prone areas — and reassigned every property a Bushfire Attack Level (BAL) rating.

Most of the Endeavour Hills Hills sit at **BAL-12.5 to BAL-29**. North-facing slopes near remnant forest hit BAL-40 or BAL-FZ. These ratings are mandatory: any new deck, pergola or alfresco structure must meet AS 3959 to the property's BAL level. The local building surveyor checks at occupancy permit stage. No sign-off = no occupancy.

Sixteen years after Black Saturday, most general carpenters still quote without checking the BAL. The client signs, the deck gets built, the surveyor rejects it, and the client now has a $25,000 deck that has to be partially rebuilt before they can move back in.

### What the BAL levels actually require

**BAL-12.5 (low-risk).** Treated pine deck OK above 400mm height with Class 1 (non-combustible) underdecking sheeting and ember-mesh skirts around the perimeter. Most Emerald and Cranbourne Upper blocks.

**BAL-19 (moderate-risk).** Hardwood decking only (spotted gum, blackbutt, jarrah, ironbark) with no gaps wider than 5mm. Class 1 underdecking. BAL-rated fasteners. Most Cockatoo lower-slope blocks.

**BAL-29 (high-risk).** Same hardwood + 5mm-max-gap rule, PLUS a non-combustible substructure (steel C-section bearers or fire-treated LVL). Plus a 1-metre ember-protection apron around the deck perimeter. Some Cockatoo upper-slope and Cranbourne Upper north-facing blocks.

**BAL-40 / BAL-FZ (very high / flame-zone).** Hardwood only above non-combustible substructure with full ember-mesh perimeter and fire-rated wall construction at the deck-house interface. Composite generally not approved. The Cockatoo north-facing slopes nearest forest, parts of Hampton Park and Gembrook ridgelines.

### Why composite isn't the BAL answer

Composite decking (Trex, Modwood, Ekodeck, Futurewood) is fantastic for the Berwick/Narre Warren growth corridor — zero maintenance, no warping, no oiling for 25+ years. We install plenty of it.

But composite doesn't pass AS 3959 above BAL-12.5 because the polymer component is combustible. Some products claim BAL-19 compliance with specific install details (continuous backing, narrow gaps) but in practice we don't use composite on Hills BAL-19+ jobs because the certification edge case isn't worth the surveyor sign-off risk.

For Endeavour Hills Hills BAL-19 or higher: it's hardwood, full stop.

### The reactive-clay footing detail nobody mentions

A separate issue from BAL: Class M reactive clay sits under most of South East Melbourne (yes, even up in the Hills in the lower-elevation pockets). Standard 600mm-deep concrete pad footings or 90mm-stub-on-pad construction will heave with seasonal clay movement and warp the deck within 3 years.

We default to **1.2m concrete pad footings** or **screw piles** driven below the seasonal active zone on all Class M and H sites. Costs an extra $150–$300 per footing. Worth every cent.

### The honest comparison: BAL-rated hardwood deck cost in 2026

A 30m² hardwood (spotted gum or blackbutt) BAL-19-compliant deck on a Cockatoo or Emerald block, properly footed and properly substructured:

- Materials: ~$11,000 (hardwood + Class 1 underdecking + BAL-rated fasteners + ember mesh)
- Substructure + footings: ~$3,500 (steel C-section or fire-treated LVL on screw piles)
- Labour: ~$8,000 (90 hours specialist work)
- Building permit + surveyor: ~$1,800
- **Total: ~$24,000**

A non-BAL-compliant treated-pine deck on the same block would run $14,000–$18,000. Cheap. Beautiful for two summers. Then the surveyor refuses to sign off the home insurance renewal because the deck doesn't meet AS 3959, and you're paying $15,000 to redo half the substructure with the deck attached to the house.

### Five questions every Endeavour Hills Hills homeowner should ask

1. **What's the BAL rating of this property?** (They should look it up on the Vic Building Authority bushfire map. If they don't know what that is, walk away.)
2. **Will this design pass AS 3959 to that BAL?** (Should be a confident yes with the specific clauses ticked off.)
3. **Who's the building surveyor?** (They should already have one lined up.)
4. **What footing depth?** (1.2m or screw piles on Class M/H sites.)
5. **What substructure material at this BAL?** (Should be specific — steel C-section, FT-LVL, hardwood-only above non-combustible.)
